Featured product

URLStrip

URLStrip removes tracking junk from links before you open them, save them, or share them with someone else.

Overview

URLStrip checks links for known tracking parameters and redirect patterns, removes what should not be there, and keeps functional parameters when they are necessary for the link to keep working.

  • Removes common trackers like utm_* and similar parameters
  • Unwraps many tracking and redirect links
  • Preserves required content and state parameters when needed
  • Lets users report missed trackers from inside the app

How it works

The desktop versions are designed to work invisibly in normal use. Copy a link, share a link, or paste a link into the app and URLStrip cleans it locally on the device. On iPhone, the app gives you a clean workflow for copied links, quick checks, QR-based intake, onboarding, settings control, and visibility into what was removed.

  • macOS app for quiet day-to-day cleanup
  • Windows app built around the same local cleaning model
  • iPhone app for quick cleaning, stats, QR intake, and setup help

How to use it

  1. Install the app for your platform.
  2. Copy or share a link as you normally would.
  3. Let URLStrip clean the tracking junk locally.
  4. Open, save, or share the cleaner result.

Some parameters are intentionally kept because they control content, restore state, or are required for the destination to function.

Rules and coverage

URLStrip is grounded in the upstream ClearURLs ruleset and then extended with additional maintained coverage where it improves practical results. That gives the project a strong public baseline instead of reinventing the wheel from scratch.

That means the app benefits from a well-known public rules base while still improving results through additional maintained coverage and product-specific tuning.
